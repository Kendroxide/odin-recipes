Belgian Waffles

These waffles are crunchy on the outside and soft in the inside. One of my favorite breakfasts on the weekend. The recipe will yeild enough for 4 servings. I usually store the reminder in a zip lock bag and can re-heat in the toaster or air fryer. Also great frozen!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white flour
  • 1 1/4 cup whole wheat flour
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • salt to taste
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 eggs, seperated
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted
  • 2 cups milk
  • Splash of vanilla

Instructions

  • Combine flour, sugar, salt, cinnamon, baking powder, and baking soda in a large bowl
  • Seperate the egg yolk from the white. Each should go in a seperate bowl
  • Using electric beaters, beat the egg whites until a stiff peak. Set aside for now
  • Mix the egg yolks, milk, butter and vanilla. Mix until smooth
  • Pour the egg, milk, vanilla and butter into the dry ingredients. Mix until there are no lumps
  • Add the egg whites and carefully fold into the batter with a spatula. Be careful not to over mix. There should be lumps of egg whites.
  • Let it sit for 15 minutes while waffle iron is heating up. It is important that you let the batter rest.
  • Pour the batter into the waffle iron and cook. I like to top this with a fruit sauce.
